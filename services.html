    What to Expect During Your Saddle Fitting Consultation

    Please note: Saddle fitting consultations can take a few hours for adjustments, or choose a quicker one-hour check-up. Please have your clean, dry horse ready with bridle, boots, girth, stirrups, and pad for a smooth session!

    Checking an Existing Saddle:

  • Initial Assessment: I'll place the saddle on your horse's back with your usual pad and observe you riding.
  • On-Site Adjustments: If minor adjustments are needed, I'll make them right there for optimal comfort.
  • Second Evaluation: You might ride again to ensure correct adjustments.
  • Advanced Work: For significant work, the saddle may go to my workshop or the manufacturer.

    • Fitting a brand New Saddle:

  • Trying Different Saddles: We'll test various saddles on your horse's back to assess the fit.
  • Rider Input: Your feedback plays a crucial role in selecting the most comfortable option.
  • Thin Pad: We'll use a thin numnah to avoid any irritation during the trial.
  • Refined Selection: After narrowing down the options, we'll do a more in-depth evaluation of the top contenders.

    Common Saddle Fit Questions


    How often should I get my saddle fitted?

    Saddle checkups depend on your horse! Young, growing horses need them every 6 months. Mature horses can wait a year, but watch for weight changes, new riding styles, or behavioral issues (bucking, rearing) - those might mean a refit is needed sooner.

    Do you travel for consultations, or do I need to bring my horse to you?

    I can travel to your stable for on-site consultations - I cover Hampshire, Wiltshire and Dorset. But please do contact me to discuss the best option for you and your horse!

    My horse is young/old/has a specific conformation. Can you still fit a saddle for him/her?

    All shapes & sizes are welcome! Whether your horse is young, old, or has a unique conformation, I've got you covered. My experience and training allows me to find the perfect fit for all my equine partners.

    I'm worried my horse won't tolerate a saddle fitting consultation.

    I understand your concern! Not all horses are comfortable with new people or procedures. I prioritise a calm and gentle approach during consultations. But please feel free to discuss your horse's specific needs or anxieties beforehand. This allows me to tailor the consultation to minimise stress.
